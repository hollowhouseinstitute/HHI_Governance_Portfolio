Portfolio Architecture (Apps 1–7)

This portfolio is organized as a sequence of applications that progress from human authority to live infrastructure and continuous governance. Each app builds on the previous layer.

App 1 — Identity & Authority Anchor

Purpose: Who owns the system and who has final authority.

  • Human-in-the-loop declaration
  • Non-autonomous operation
  • Explicit ownership and intent

App 2 — Governance Glossary

Purpose: Language control before capability.

  • Canonical, upstream definitions
  • Terms govern behavior, not the reverse
  • No agent behavior without language lock

App 3 — Decision Boundaries

Purpose: What the system may and may not do.

  • Explicit constraints
  • No inference beyond declared scope
  • Boundary visibility

App 4 — Stop Authority

Purpose: How execution halts.

  • Human stop power
  • Non-bypassable halt conditions
  • Meaningful STOP mechanism

App 5 — Escalation & Audit Trail

Purpose: What happens when limits are reached.

  • Escalation logs
  • Traceable decisions
  • Inspection over automation

App 6 — Deployment Proof (Cloud Run)

Purpose: Infrastructure can enforce governance.

  • Live deployment
  • Inspectable runtime
  • Inspection-only posture

App 7 — Continuous Aftercare

Purpose: Governance over time.

  • Drift detection
  • Override erosion signals
  • Longitudinal accountability

Summary: The portfolio progresses from human authority → language control → boundaries → stop power → auditability → live infrastructure → continuous governance.

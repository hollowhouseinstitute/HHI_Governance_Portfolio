Governance Portfolio

Purpose

This portfolio demonstrates a governance-first framework for evaluating AI systems across time rather than snapshot performance. Governance is treated as infrastructure: ordered, dependency-bound, and enforceable.

Human–AI Co-Creation Model

AI systems are used as co-creators under explicit constraints. They assist with synthesis and structure but do not hold authority.

AI Job Description

  • Synthesis and drafting assistance
  • Structural and language consistency checks
  • Option surfacing without outcome selection

Explicit Non-Responsibilities

  • No decision authority
  • No scope determination
  • No autonomous execution

Why Framework Order Matters

Governance failures often occur not because controls are missing, but because they are applied out of order.

Authority precedes language.
Language precedes boundaries.
Boundaries precede execution.
Stop power precedes escalation.
Infrastructure enforces declared limits.

Identity & Authority Anchor

No automated decisions are performed. No component may override human judgment.

AI as Co-Creator (Defined Role)

AI systems were used as co-creators under explicit constraints. Their role is limited, observable, and non-authoritative.

AI Job Description

  • Structural reasoning across governance artifacts
  • Language consistency checks against canonical definitions
  • Draft synthesis without authority escalation

AI systems did not make decisions, enforce rules, or operate autonomously. All authority, acceptance, and rejection remained human-governed.

Portfolio Architecture (Apps 1–7)

This portfolio is ordered intentionally. Each layer depends on the one before it. Reordering breaks governance.

  1. Identity & Authority Anchor — who owns decisions
  2. Governance Glossary — language before capability
  3. Decision Boundaries — explicit scope limits
  4. Stop Authority — non-bypassable halt
  5. Escalation & Audit Trail — inspection over automation

  7. Continuous Aftercare — governance over time
